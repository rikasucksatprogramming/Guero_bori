War Against All Puerto Ricans: Revolution and Terror in America's colony

A “War Against all Puerto Ricans: Revolution and Terror in America’s colony”, was a great book to showcase the unheard stories of Puerto Ricans,
the nationalist party, and our forgotten history. Robert Dominguez from the New York Times says after reading, “Prepared to be outraged”,
as this book is filled with topics that will anger any Puerto Rican, from how the Americans spoke about us, to why we are so proud of our flag.
This book is a very hard read as it is very descriptive but a very worthy read. This book will completely flip your world when it comes to the US and Puerto Rico.
Synopsis: “War against all Puerto Ricans” was divided into 3 sections with a few chapters in each,
the first section being Facts, second being people, and third being Events. The first section corrects
many misconceptions that a America centered media made, such as the fact that the Puerto Ricans were
shooting police during the Ponce massacre while it was the complete opposite, it also talks about how
the Americans failed with demonizing Spanish in the island, and much more. The second section dove into
the important trailblazers and not so helpful people of the island. First diving into the weak governor
who gave the Americans complete control of Puerto Rico for his own good, then into the trailblazers such
as Pedro Albizu Campos, “The Nationalist” and el barbero. The last section talked about the fall of the
Nationalist party as all their leaders were killed and were scared. The US was able to unleash propogranda
to effectively control the island and essentially make people forget about the Nationalist movement or all
of their leadership failures, leading us to today.. well 2015.

Pedro Albizu Campos

Pedro Albizu Campos was the biggest pioneer for the
Nationalist Party in Puerto Rico being a former
lawyer and even serving the US before going on to lead the Nationalist
movement. He educated many Puerto Ricans at the time to the
issues going on in the Island and what the best way forward is.

Blanca Canales

Canales was a major member of the Nationalist party especially as a women.
She lead the Jayuya Uprising, showing her true commitment to the island.

Vidal Santiago "el barbero" Diaz

Believe or not Vidal Santiago Diaz was a barber but despite being Pedro Albizu Campos’
barber he served a very important part
in the nationalist revolution. He ran the salon
boricua which was a very popular meeting place for
nationalists, to the point where the
FBI pulled up and kidnapped him for his knowledge about Campos.
